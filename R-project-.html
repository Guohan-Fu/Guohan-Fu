The variables that should be reviewed in order to validate the recommendation are: View product quantity over a specific period of time (e.g., each month). See how product prices change as the month progresses. Check out which months there are high-priced products for sale. The Data Source

uksale = uk_retail %>% filter(Country == 'United Kingdom')
library(lubridate)
uksale$Month = month(mdy(uksale$Date))
uksale$Month = factor(uksale$Month)

What is the relationship between the month and price in the past year in United Kingdom?

library(ggplot2)
ggplot(data = uksale) +
  geom_point(aes(x = Month, y = Price, color = Price)) +
  labs(x = 'Month',y = 'price',title = "Comparison of month and price changes over the past year")


From the figure above, it can be analysed that the change in the month will have an impact on the price. For example, in the first half of the year, especially during the period from March to June, there are products with prices over $200 and even some products with prices between $600 and $650. In the second half of the year, there were no products priced over $200. This data is very informative for consumers to avoid overpaying or to promote them to buy when prices are low.


This also provides support for the later validation of the correlation between volume and price.
